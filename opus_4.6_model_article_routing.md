## Summary and Analysis

### Overall Pattern

Of ~210+ Knowledge Hub articles reviewed:

- **~140 articles (67%) → Category A: Better for Opus**
- **~70+ articles (33%) → Category B: Standard Workflow**

The high proportion of Opus-suitable articles reflects the nature of the property management industry in the DC/VA/MD market: the regulatory environment is unusually complex (three jurisdictions, rent control, TOPA, extensive HOA statutes), and much of the content that performs well for SEO requires legal precision to be genuinely useful and authoritative.

### Article Clusters That Clearly Fit "Better for Opus"

1. **HOA Legal/Governance Cluster (~53 articles):** The single largest Opus cluster. Nearly every HOA article requires multi-jurisdiction statutory citations (VA POAA, DC Condo Act, MD HOA Act), fiduciary duty analysis, or governance procedure guidance. This cluster includes enforcement guides, foreclosure procedures, election law, reserve study requirements, and board orientation content.

2. **DC Rent Control & Tenant Law Cluster (~15 articles):** Rent control (§ 42-3501), TOPA (§ 42-3404), Tenant Bill of Rights (§ 42-3502.22), Fair Criminal Record Screening Act (§ 2-1402.68), and eviction procedures require precise statutory language and cannot be approximated.

3. **Security Deposit & Lease Law Cluster (~10 articles):** Three different deposit regimes (DC 1-month cap, VA 2-month cap, MD varies), different interest requirements, different penalty structures, and different return timelines make these inherently complex.

4. **Licensing & Compliance Cluster (~8 articles):** DC BBL/DLCP, VA DPOR, MD MREC, and county-level licensing requirements for both landlords and property managers.

5. **Commercial Lease & CRE Cluster (~10 articles):** Commercial lease structures, tax strategies, BEPS compliance, and due diligence processes require depth.

6. **Tax & Financial Depth Cluster (~8 articles):** Property tax appeals, 1031 exchanges, LLC formation, and investment mortgage analysis.

7. **Foundational PM Resource Pages (~10 articles):** Cornerstone "how to choose a PM" and "ultimate guide" pages need comprehensive treatment to serve as anchor pages.

### Borderline Categories Where Judgment Was Required

1. **Mold/Pest articles:** Classified split — articles focused primarily on remediation techniques → Standard; articles centered on landlord liability and habitability law → Opus. The distinguishing factor is whether the article's future value comes from legal precision or practical tips.

2. **Maintenance guides with regulatory sections:** Spring/summer/fall maintenance checklists are Standard Workflow even though they now contain legal citations. The legal content is secondary to the practical advice. However, HVAC responsibility articles landed in Opus because the legal question (who is responsible) is the core of the article.

3. **Investment comparison articles:** Articles like "Fix and Flip vs. Renting" and "Cash vs. Financing" were classified as Opus because they require nuanced financial analysis with DC rent control and TOPA implications. Simpler investment articles ("Is Investing Wise?" "Common Mistakes") stayed Standard.

4. **HOA community/social articles:** Social committee guides, newsletter creation, and curb appeal tips were classified as Standard despite being HOA content. They don't require legal depth — they need tighter, more engaging writing.

5. **Neighborhood guide articles:** All ~20 neighborhood-specific "Property Management in DC: Living in [Neighborhood]" articles are Standard Workflow. They need fresh, commercially aligned local content, not legal analysis.

### Recommendations for Routing Article Updates Going Forward

1. **Default to Opus for any article that cites statutes.** If the article currently contains DC Code, VRLTA, or MD Real Property citations, or should contain them, route it to Opus. Legal precision is non-negotiable in this market.

2. **Default to Standard Workflow for any article whose primary value is commercial.** Tenant attraction, marketing tips, seasonal maintenance, neighborhood guides, and conversion-supporting content should sound natural, not exhaustive.

3. **Route new HOA content to Opus by default.** The HOA service line is inherently governance-heavy and statute-dependent. Even "lighter" HOA topics like newsletters and curb appeal will occasionally touch enforcement authority or board fiduciary duty.

4. **Use Standard Workflow for new geo/market articles.** Content about specific DC metro neighborhoods, rental market trends, and investment opportunity areas benefits from tighter, more current-sounding writing.

5. **For borderline articles, ask: "What kind of rewrite quality does this article need most?"**
   - If the answer is "more legally precise and comprehensive" → Opus
   - If the answer is "tighter, more natural, more commercially compelling" → Standard Workflow

6. **Consider splitting complex articles.** Some current articles try to serve both legal/educational and conversion purposes. In future updates, consider whether the article should be split into a deep Opus-quality resource page and a shorter Standard Workflow conversion page.

7. **New content types by workflow:**
   - Opus: jurisdiction-specific law guides, board education series, compliance checklists, reserve/insurance deep dives, new commercial lease explainers
   - Standard: seasonal tips, tenant attraction, property upgrade ROI, neighborhood investment spotlights, service comparison pages, client testimonial-supporting content
