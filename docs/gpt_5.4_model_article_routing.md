The strongest `Better for Opus` pattern was concentrated in HOA governance/policy content, landlord law/compliance content, commercial lease and finance explainers, and a smaller set of true cornerstone guides. The larger `Standard workflow` pattern was geo-market content, practical owner tips, maintenance/amenity articles, neighborhood guides, and conversion-supporting service articles.

- HOA board governance, reserve, rules, enforcement, and contract pages: 23 articles.
- Landlord law/compliance pages: 19 articles.
- Foundational landlord and commercial resource pages: 9 articles.
- Nuanced pricing, ownership, and investment comparison pages: 6 articles.
- Analytical operational thought-leadership pages: 3 articles.

Borderline categories where judgment was required:
- Decision-stage property-management selection pages were generally kept in `Standard workflow` unless the topic clearly turned into a law/compliance or deep tradeoff page.
- Operational HOA pages were split: governance, policy, enforcement, reserve, and contract pages went to `Better for Opus`, while lighter community-operations pages stayed `Standard workflow`.
- Investment and finance content was routed to `Better for Opus` only when it read as a foundational guide, a nuanced comparison, or a tax/compliance explainer; otherwise it stayed `Standard workflow`.

Recommendations for routing going forward:
- Use `Better for Opus` for jurisdiction-specific landlord law, HOA governance/policy, commercial lease structure, reserve/budget/contract education, and true cornerstone guides.
- Keep service-adjacent, geo, market, amenity, maintenance, and lighter investor-support articles in `Standard workflow` so they stay tighter, more natural, and more commercially aligned.
- When a page is both high-intent and high-complexity, route based on the harder part of the rewrite. If precision and nuance matter more than brevity, use `Better for Opus`.
- For future content planning, treat `Better for Opus` pages as pillar-resource candidates and refresh them less often but more deeply; treat `Standard workflow` pages as the faster-moving editorial layer.
